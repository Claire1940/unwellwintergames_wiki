One of the most difficult challenges in the winter zones involves reflecting light off frozen mirrors to melt a central ice core. You must align the mirrors in a specific sequence while fending off waves of minor shadows.

1.  **Locate the Primary Beam:** Find the light source entering from the ceiling.
2.  **Clean the Mirrors:** Use a cloth to remove the frost from the reflective surfaces.
3.  **Rotate the Pedestals:** Align the light so it hits the mirrors in a clockwise fashion.
4.  **Protect the Core:** Stay within the light beam to keep your frostbite gauge low during the process.

**Q: Can I use the standard torch instead of the lantern?**

A: Torches provide more heat but have a much shorter duration and smaller light radius. They are best used for quick room clearing rather than long-term exploration.
