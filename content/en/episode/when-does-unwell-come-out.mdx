## Core Gameplay Mechanics and Sanity Systems

*Unwell* is not a traditional horror game where combat is the primary focus. Instead, it utilizes an isometric perspective to create a sense of claustrophobia and vulnerability. The core loop revolves around exploration, resource management, and maintaining your character's mental state.

### The Sanity Meter
The most innovative feature of *Unwell* is the Sanity Meter. Unlike standard health bars, sanity fluctuates based on lighting, proximity to Eldritch horrors, and the duration spent in the "Lower Wells." As your sanity drops, the world around you changes—walls may appear to bleed, whispers will fill the audio channels, and certain paths may physically alter themselves.

| Sanity Level | Visual/Audio Effects | Gameplay Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stable (80-100%)** | Clear vision, steady heartbeat | Normal movement speed; high accuracy |
| **Unsettled (50-79%)** | Slight peripheral blurring; distant whispers | Increased stamina consumption |
| **Disturbed (20-49%)** | Hallucinations; color desaturation | Interaction prompts may be false |
| **Broken (0-19%)** | Heavy distortion; screaming audio | Random character freezing; high death risk |

### Exploration and Navigation
Navigating the well requires a keen eye for detail. The levels are procedurally generated to an extent, meaning that while key story rooms remain in fixed locations, the corridors connecting them shift. This ensures that even veteran players feel a sense of unease during every playthrough. You must use a limited supply of torches and oil lamps to keep the darkness at bay, as staying in total darkness for more than 30 seconds results in an immediate and drastic sanity penalty.

## Character Progression and Survival Crafting

## Expert Strategies for Navigating the Well

Surviving in *Unwell* requires a shift in mindset compared to standard action-horror games. Stealth is almost always preferable to combat. The enemies in the well, known as "The Afflicted," react to sound and light. Learning how to manipulate these elements is the key to progress.

### Stealth and Sound Management
Every action you take produces noise. Running on metal grates is much louder than walking on dirt. You can throw stones or glass bottles to distract enemies, creating a window for you to slip past. 

1. **Crouch-walk by default:** Unless you are being actively chased, there is no reason to run.
2. **Manage your light:** If you see an enemy patrol, extinguish your lamp and hide in a corner. Wait for their light signature to fade before moving.
3. **Map your exits:** Before entering a large room, identify at least two ways out. The well often collapses behind you, forcing you to find alternative routes.

### Managing the Protagonist's Health
Healing in *Unwell* is a slow process. Using a bandage takes several seconds of standing still, during which you are completely vulnerable. It is often better to retreat to a "Safe Room" (indicated by a blue lantern) before attempting to mend major wounds.

## FAQ

**Q: When does unwell come out for Nintendo Switch?**

A: Currently, the developers have focused on PC and high-end consoles. A Nintendo Switch port has been discussed for late 2026, but no official release date has been set. The team wants to ensure the lighting effects are properly optimized for the handheld hardware first.

**Q: Is Unwell a multiplayer game?**

A: No, *Unwell* is strictly a single-player experience. The developers have stated that adding other players would diminish the sense of isolation and psychological dread that is central to the game's atmosphere.

**Q: Are there multiple endings in Unwell?**

A: Yes, the 1.0 version features four distinct endings based on your Sanity levels at key story beats and the number of Memory Shards you have collected. Your choices regarding which NPCs to help (or sacrifice) also play a significant role in the final outcome.

**Q: Does the game support controllers on PC?**

A: Absolutely. *Unwell* features full controller support and a redesigned UI specifically for gamepads, making the isometric movement feel fluid and intuitive for those who prefer playing on a couch.
