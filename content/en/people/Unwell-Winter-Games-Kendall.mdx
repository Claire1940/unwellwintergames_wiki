## Hunting Tactics and Strategies

Mastering the **Unwell Winter Games Kendall** requires more than just good aim; it requires an understanding of animal AI and environmental factors. In 2026, the animal behavior in Silver Ridge Peaks has been noted as "unusually aggressive," with predators often stalking the hunter.

### The Aggressive Predator Approach
When hunting Mountain Lions or Black Bears, be prepared for a charge. If an animal becomes "Attentive" or "Alert" at close range, switch to your sidearm. The 10mm Glock is the preferred tool for stopping a charging bear mid-stride. Aim for the chest or head to ensure a quick drop.

### The Bison "School Bus" Tactic
Bison are the tanks of the Rocky Mountains. They are large targets, but their thick hide and heavy bone structure make them difficult to take down with a single shot. If using a bow, ensure you are using 700-grain arrows for maximum penetration. If the first shot doesn't down the animal, be ready to track the blood trail for several hundred yards.

### Long-Range Sniping for Bighorn and Goats
Bighorn Sheep and Mountain Goats inhabit the steepest parts of the map. Finding them often requires hours of glassing with binoculars. Once spotted, use a prone position to stabilize your rifle. Shots at these altitudes often involve significant windage and elevation adjustments.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid shooting turkeys with high-caliber rifles if you want to preserve the trophy rating. A .303 will "vaporize" a turkey, resulting in a failed harvest check.

## Tracking and Trophy Management

A key component of the **Unwell Winter Games Kendall** is the quality of the harvest. While the goal is to kill every animal, achieving Gold or Diamond ratings is the ultimate mark of a pro hunter. Use the "Blood Hound" companion, Jamal, to track wounded animals that flee into thick brush.

| Trophy Rating | Requirements |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Bronze** | Basic harvest with at least one vital hit. |
| **Silver** | Proper caliber used, vital organ hit, and less than 2 shots. |
| **Gold** | High trophy score (horns/weight) and perfect harvest checks. |
| **Diamond** | Maximum trophy score and flawless execution. |

In the 2026 circuit, many players have struggled with the Bighorn Sheep. These animals often require a "double lung" shot to drop instantly. If the bullet stops in the "flesh" or "meat," the trophy rating will likely drop to Silver, even if the animal is eventually harvested.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the main objective of the Unwell Winter Games Kendall?**

A: The objective is to harvest one of every animal species on the Silver Ridge Peaks map, usually following the high-energy and opportunistic hunting style popularized by Kendall Gray.

**Q: Can I use any weapon for the challenge?**

A: While you can use any legal weapon, the challenge traditionally encourages a mix of the .303 Sporter for range, the Glock 10mm for defense, and the Recurve Bow for a "traditional" feel.

**Q: Why are my trophy ratings always Silver?**

A: To get Gold or Diamond, you must pass all four harvest checks: using the correct ammo class, hitting a vital organ (heart, lung, liver, or neck spine), not damaging the trophy organ (usually the head/skull), and downing the animal in two shots or fewer.

**Q: Is there a time limit for the 2026 Unwell Winter Games?**

A: Most community-driven "Unwell" challenges are self-paced, though some competitive versions during the 2026 season may have specific leaderboards or time-frames.

**Q: What should I do if a Bear charges me?**

A: Do not panic. Switch to a high-capacity sidearm like the 10mm handgun and fire rapidly at the center mass. In the **Unwell Winter Games Kendall**, survival is just as important as the harvest.
